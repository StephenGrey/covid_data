Read our full investigation here: Into the fog: How Britain lost track of the coronavirus


Infection was spreading widely in the UK long before people started dying from COVID-19. With inconsistent and low volumes of testing for COVID-19, the best method available for working out infections is working back from deaths that follow approximately 3 weeks later.

Notes:

* Estimated new infections [LEFT AXIS]: a Reuters rough estimate based on a 1% infection fatality rate (IFR) [Most studies currently show IFR at 0.5%-1%] and average time from infection to death of approximately 3 weeks . The IFR will vary based on age distribution, so the number of infections is likely an over-estimate in the later stages of the outbreak when, in most areas, infection spread into care homes.

* Tests with a positive COVID-19 result [RIGHT AXIS]: these are tests carried out in Public Health England, NHS labs, and private "Lighthouse" labs. Updated 1/7/20 with tests in all labs with new PHE data.

* Deaths [RIGHT AXIS]: derived from death certificates where COVID-19 is listed as a cause of death. Location is by usual place of residence (not place of death). This figure is significantly below recorded excess deaths for the time of year, suggesting the COVID-19 figures are an under-estimate.

* All estimates are unreliable in areas with few deaths, e.g. the City of London.

Source: Office of National Statistics; National Records of Scotland; Northern Ireland Statistics and Research Agency; Public Health England; Public Health Wales; Scottish government.
